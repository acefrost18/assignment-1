Polar Bear

Polar bears are exceptional swimmers, capable of covering long distances in the frigid waters of the Arctic Ocean. Their main prey is seals, which they catch by waiting near breathing holes in the ice or ambushing them at seal breathing cracks. These hunting skills are essential for their survival in the challenging Arctic ecosystem. One of the most captivating aspects of polar bears is their close association with the sea ice, which serves as both a platform for hunting seals and a place for resting and giving birth. Female polar bears create dens in the snow and ice to give birth and nurture their cubs, typically giving birth to one or two cubs at a time. Unfortunately, polar bears face an existential threat due to the ongoing loss of their sea ice habitat caused by climate change. With the reduction of sea ice, polar bears have to cover longer distances to find food, leading to increased challenges in their survival. Conservation efforts and international cooperation are crucial to protecting these magnificent creatures and preserving the Arctic ecosystem they call home.
